Mercedes- Benz A-Class

The Mercedes-Benz A-Class is a compact car produced by the German automobile manufacturer Mercedes-Benz. All models are powered by four-cylinder engines, with 1.4 L and 1.6 L petrol models at launch (M 166 series), followed by two versions of a 1.7 L diesel engine (OM 668 series). In 1999, a larger 1.9 L petrol model was added, with the 2.1 the last W168 version to be launched in 2002.

Between 1997 and 2004, 1.1 million first generation A-Class models had been sold.

First generation (W168; 1997–2004)
Second generation (W169; 2004–2013)
Third generation (W176; 2012–present)

Mercedes- Benz C-Class

Mercedes- Benz E-Class

The Mercedes-Benz E-Class is a range of executive cars manufactured by German automaker Mercedes-Benz, in various engine and body configurations produced since 1993. 4-cylinder, 2.1-liter turbodiesel unit with 134 hp (100 kW). The same engine powers the E220 CDI where it's tuned to output 168 hp (125 kW), There are also BlueTec versions of the E250, E300 Hybrid and E350.

Mercedes- Benz G-Class

The Mercedes-Benz G-Class, sometimes called G-Wagen, is a mid-size four-wheel drive luxury SUV, manufactured by Magna Steyr in Austria and sold by Mercedes-Benz. All models are powered by four-cylinder engines, with 1.4 L and 1.6 L petrol models at launch (M 166 series), followed by two versions of a 1.7 L diesel engine (OM 668 series). In 1999, a larger 1.9 L petrol model was added, with the 2.1 the last W168 version to be launched in 2002.

Mercedes- Benz R-Class

The R-Class was a luxury MPV offered by Daimler AG in 2006 model year under the Mercedes-Benz brand. It is the first full-sized multi-purpose vehicle ever produced by Mercedes-Benz. All models are powered by four-cylinder engines, with 1.4 L and 1.6 L petrol models at launch (M 166 series), followed by two versions of a 1.7 L diesel engine (OM 668 series). In 1999, a larger 1.9 L petrol model was added, with the 2.1 the last W168 version to be launched in 2002. The R-Class is offered in two wheelbases, 2,980 mm (117.3 in) and 3,215 mm (126.6 in), the latter being the sole offering in the US.

Mercedes- Benz V-Class

The Mercedes-Benz Vito is a light van produced by Mercedes-Benz. It is available as a standard panel van for cargo (called Vito), or with passenger accommodations substituted for part or all of the load area (called V-Class or Viano).
